About RetireCalc

Helping Americans plan for a secure retirement with free, accessible tools since 2024.

Our Mission

RetireCalc was created to make retirement planning accessible to everyone, regardless of income or financial literacy. We believe that understanding your financial future shouldn't require a degree in finance or an expensive meeting with a financial advisor. Our free, easy-to-use calculator gives you instant insight into your retirement readiness in under 60 seconds.

Millions of Americans are unsure if they're on track for retirement. According to the Federal Reserve, nearly 40% of non-retired adults feel their retirement savings are not on track. We built RetireCalc to close that gap — providing clear, actionable numbers that help you make informed decisions about your financial future.

Our Story

RetireCalc was founded by a small team of financial analysts and software engineers who recognized a common problem: most retirement planning tools are either overly complicated, require creating an account, or cost money. The calculators offered by major financial institutions are often designed to sell products, not to provide unbiased guidance.

We decided to build something different — a completely free, privacy-focused retirement calculator that does exactly what it promises: tells you if you're on track for retirement. No account required. No upsells. No data collection. Just a straightforward, accurate calculation you can trust.

Since launching, RetireCalc has helped thousands of users estimate their retirement savings and plan for a more secure financial future. We continue to improve our tools based on user feedback and the latest financial research.

How Our Calculator Works

Our retirement calculator uses standard financial formulas to project your savings growth over time. It takes into account your current age, planned retirement age, current savings, monthly contributions, expected annual return, and inflation rate. All calculations are performed locally in your browser using JavaScript — no data is sent to any server.

The calculator provides five key metrics: total retirement savings at retirement, total contributions you will make, investment growth (earnings on your investments), inflation-adjusted value (purchasing power in today's dollars), and estimated monthly retirement income based on the widely accepted 4% withdrawal rule.
